Moxibustion for good health

October 2, 2020

Mugukboyangtm is composed of 8 kinds of acupuncture points. Including the both side of body, 12 points for men and 13 points for women. One can burn moxa 3~5 times in a row per each point. It is a life curing method that anyone can use with or without a sickness or disease. Click for "How to do Moxibustion"

Meaning of Mugukboyangtm(MGBYT)

Mugukboyangtm is a moxibustion therapy developed by Gudang(Namsoo, Kim) which has proved its efficacy through numerous patients for over 60 years after it was invented by examining almost all of the moxibustions recorded in the ancient medicine books and was verified by more than 20 years of clinical practices. This is more meaningful because it can be done by anyone as well as by a practitioner. Previously, moxibustion had been limited to professional practitioners, but MGBYT is for anyone who can use it for any disease. The reason why MGBYT is both a therapeutic and curing(nourishing) method is that the essential spots of the meridian system are chosen. Each point has been selected from the most important points among 360 acupuncture points, according to the principles of harmony. Just as harmony and compatibility between people are important, so are the same between acupuncture points. There is not much disease that does not heal when one does moxibustion for a long time. MGBYT is a treatment method for chronic diseases and also for preventing illness in advance. A person with a disease needs this kind of treatment to get better and a healthy person needs this to stay healthy.

Meaning of each point of Mugukboyangtm

Keeping our body strong is in eating and digesting well. The acupuncture and moxibustion medicine says that the function of eating and digesting well is controlled by spleen and stomach. Since ancient times, it has been said that it is a measure of health as “to eat well, to sleep well, to excrete well.” The first thing to consider here is “to eat well” which is facilitated by Jung-wan(CV12), Gok-ji(LI11) and Jok-sam-ni(ST36) point of MGBYT. The other 5 points strengthen and nourish visceras. Gi-hae(CV6) and Gwan-won(CV4) to strengthen the function of kidney which charges control of the source of life and reproduction, Pye-yu(BL13) to strengthen the function of respiratory system, Go-hwang(BL43) to strengthen the function of the heart and used to cure the disease of deep inside of the body. And Baek-hoe(GV20) to strengthen the function of heart which controls the mind. Our bodies are inseparable from our minds. No wonder the body gets sick when the brain, the head of the mind, is sick. Therefore, Baek-hoe is used, at which all the yang energy gathered in the head.

In the case of women, Jung-geuk(CV3) and Su-do(ST28) are used instead of Gi-hae(CV6) and Gwan-won(CV4). Women have the physiological characteristics of menstruation which consists of liquid(water). The word “Su(水,water) Do(道,road,way)” means watery passage and Su-do points are located on the ovary. And Jung-geuk to help diuretic action and associated with uterus function(located on the uterus). And kidney function is weaker as people get older, so usually add Shin-yu(BL23) to help keep kidney healthy although this point is not included in Mugukboyangtm. As you may know, you can burn moxa on the front part of your body by yourself! Some people try the back of the body with mirror and tweezers.
